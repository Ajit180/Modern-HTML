Question 2: What are HTML Entities?List out 5 Commonly used HTML entities.

Solutions:


     HTML Entities:
                     HTML entities are special codes used to represent characters that have a specific meaning in HTML,such as reserved characters (<, >), special characters (&), and non-breaking spaces. Here are some common HTML entities


      5 Commonly Used HTML Entities:
       
1. `&lt;` - Less Than Sign `<`
   - Represents the less than sign (<).

2. `&gt;` - Greater Than Sign `>`
   - Represents the greater than sign (>).

3. `&amp;` - Ampersand `&`
   - Represents the ampersand character (&).

4. `&quot;` - Double Quote `"`
   - Represents the double quote character (").

5. `&copy;` - Copyright Symbol ©
   - Represents the copyright symbol (©).
